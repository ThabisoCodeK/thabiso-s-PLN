My Communication Tools

WhatsApp

I use whatsapp to communicate with my coursemates and I join tutorial groups for the set of tutorials I take. In this groups we share resources, sometimes past exam papers. I also communicate with my private individuals through this communication platform. I do not interact with my learners through whatsapp knowing how chaotic they can get.

Discord

Discord is used in my Methodology and Practicum Business Studies as a way to communicate other than the means of the learning management system. We receive communication and interact with other students in these communities - share resources and consult the lecturer in the module.

Google Meet

Google Meet is my go-to platform for virtual classes and online tutoring sessions. The seamless integration with Google Calendar makes scheduling sessions effortless, and the screen sharing feature allows me to present slides and demonstrate concepts effectively. The recording feature is particularly useful for learners who miss live sessions however I do not use it that much to enforce attendance with my Accounting Group.

GMail

I use Google Mail to communicate with my learners and other people I communicate. The interesting feature I use of Google Mail is the ability to schedule emails because I sometimes forget to reply promptly to the email when I immediately get it so this feature helps me alot.

My Assessment Tools

Google Forms

I use Google Forms to create assessments for the learners that are in my classes, also to collect feedback and reflection points from them. The greatest advantage is self-grading for the objective assessments - it gives me less time marking and going through everything that learners typed, they also benefit from the immediate feedback.

Kahoot

Kahoot is my go to platform for making fun and interactive learning assessments. They strike some positive competition in learners to reach the podium which is the fun part of it. I also collaborated with my colleagues in Life Sciences to curate an assessment for their learners. I also have done some assessments evident for my methodologies which I have engaged learners in recently.

Mentimeter

I use Mentimeter to create interactive assessments for Business Studies learners. Mentimeter allows for me to hold QnA sessions after the assessments have been concluded to get learners ideas. I also share these with other colleagues in this subject methodologies to implement in their class, I collaborated with another colleague to do the Grade 10 Contemporary Socio-Economic Issues Assessment which is the fun of this tool.

Gimkit

Gimkit provides me with self-paced assessments that students can complete individually or in discord. I appreciate the detailed reports that help me track student progress and identify learning gaps. The memes and avatars make the assessment experience enjoyable for students, while the comprehensive question bank saves me time in creating assessments.

My Content Curation Tools

Pinterest

I use Pinterest to curate boards across all the subjects that I teach and those of interest. I collect teaching resources such as visuals for business studies, accounting, ems and physical education. I store them and share them with learners when they need supplementary resources. For Physical Education, I collect game-ideas and teaching strategies across all subjects.

Symbaloo

I use Symbaloo to organise all the websites that I use into a Webmix that is very interactive. I also allow my learners access to the webmix when I have important resources that I have shared on the webmix to encourage them to be online instead of me feeding them resources all the time.

Padlet

I use Padlet to create collaborative boards to plan my lessons and create assessment opportunities that are interactive. I enjoy this app for setting up my lessons and breaking it into small effective sessions with number of activities embedded in the lesson. Padlet uses a very cool AI tool that allows one to plan and remove ineffective teaching activities in the lesson plan.

Quizlet

Quizlet is a platform I use to create flashcards for content heavy subjects such as Business Studies and EMS. I create flashcards that learners can use to study and even practice concepts with. I have created flashcards for Business Studies and EMS evident below.

Education.com

I use Education.com to curate crossword puzzles for my grade 8 and 9 learners. I used this in class to test their recall of the concepts that they have learnt - the lowest level of the Bloom's taxonomy. This cross-word puzzles are very fun to do and in all the classes I implemented them during WIL, learners seemed to enjoy the activities.

My Teaching Tools

Google Classroom

I use Google Classroom with my Grade 12 Business Studies and Accounting learners that I tutor, I am able to give them activities and give updates on upcoming activities. Although I normally do not use this to mark assessments, I give learners activities via this platform and they are able to submit online and we discuss the activities when we interact in person.

Nearpod

Nearpod transforms my traditional presentations into interactive learning experiences. I use it to create lessons with embedded quizzes, polls, and collaborative boards that keep students engaged. The real-time feedback and assessment features help me gauge student understanding instantly and adjust my teaching accordingly.

PowerPoint

`

I use Powerpoint to create visual content for learners. I use it with conjuction with Nearpod where I include the activities for learners and generate questions. I make available to learners the slides most of the times after our interactions.

My Online Communities

edWeb

I use edWeb to connect with other teachers across the globe who are passionate about pursuing the idea of teaching in the 21st century. I join webinars in which classroom management strategies, assessment curation and a whole lot of other important education resources are shared and discussed. I also joined some communities with the broader community about the teaching of the 21st century skills and how to incorporate GBL (Game-Based Learning). I also earned a certificate for participation and engagement in webinars hosted by experts in the field of education.

Edutopia

The Edutopia community is very useful for when asking questions regarding teaching - how to integrate technology, classroom management amongst other aspects. My interaction includes responding to questions by other teachers that I think I am knowledgeable in, bookmarking ideas for future use in the classroom or any articles that speak to my subject methodologies. I also ask questions for classroom implementation.

PE Buddy

PE Buddy is a community of Physical Education and Sports teachers around the world including countries such as the USA where PE is prioritised. Although it is not about making posts, its about sharing resources that improve the teaching of PE. I have used some of the resources to prepare for my lessons on Pickleball and teaching the lesson on health and safety related issues on Physical Education. I also use the ebook from the community amongst some other resources to shape my teaching as a pre-service teacher.

Teachers Connect

I use Teachers Connect to complete courses offered by the Department of Basic Education for teacher developnment in curriculum and the highly spoken about Project-Based Learning. I have completed these courses about curriculum in the Senior Phase & FET to understand curriculum in these phases.

My Learning Websites

YouTube

I use YouTube for my own professional development as a teacher, I learn new content in my methodologies and areas of interest. I use Youtube as a way to enhance my Pedagogical Content Knowledge and keeps me abreast with changes in the way of teaching particular subjects. I watch videos that relate to the fields of Accounting and Physical Education. I also use YouTube to learn technologies that I believe will be beneficial for the future.

freeCodeCamp

I use freeCodeCamp to learn how to code (my personal development and learning) and I also got a little certificate from them, they provide practical learning for coding and they provide a certificate of completion after satisfying the necessary requirements. I am currently learning Javascript and Python with them.

learn-accounting

Learn Accounting is a free website that I use to learn accounting to advance the knowledge that I already have. The website is self-paced and gives assessment opportunities for every topic that is covered. There are supplementary readings for all topics around 16 topics and over 20 study videos.

w3schools

I utilize W3Schools to supplement my learning of computer engineering technologies which are some of my interests. I use it closely with freeCodeCamp to get perspectives from both platforms to ensure that I do not learn outdated versions of the technologies.

My Cloud Storages

Google Drive

I use Google Drive to manage my teaching resources that I use, for example below there is a folder for Accounting across the four grades. This gives me a clean and a seamlessly accessible folder when I need to use it. It also allows me to share content with my learners. Dropbox

I use Dropbox as an additional cloud storage solution for backing up my editing projects for my side quests. I use it also to store my lesson plans in a different folder. OneDrive

I use it to store all the assignments I type and assessments that I create for learners and the Powerpoints that I create.
